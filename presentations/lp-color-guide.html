Why Color Matters

01
Trust & Authority

Color choices signal professionalism before a single word is read. Inconsistent palettes erode buyer confidence instantly.

02
Brand Recognition

Consistent color increases brand recall by up to 80%. Luxury brokerages live and die on this — every pixel is brand.

03
User Experience

Color guides attention, creates hierarchy, and drives conversion. The wrong accent color on a CTA costs real leads.

02 / 11
System

The Color Tier System

Every LP site palette follows this three-tier architecture — no exceptions.

Foundation
60 – 70%
Brand Neutrals
Navy, white, charcoal, cream — the dominant visual mass of every page
Support
20 – 30%
Secondary Tones
Warm grays, soft blues, mid-tones — supporting texture without competing
Accent
10 – 15%
Active / Highlight
One bold brand color — used only for CTAs, active states, and key UI moments
03 / 11
Do

Use Brand Color
with Intent

  • Apply accent color to primary CTAs, active nav states, and key UI moments only
  • Keep accent coverage under 15% of total visible page real estate
  • Let neutral backgrounds do the heavy lifting — they make the accent pop
  • One bold color per page; grays and whites are structural, not accents

04 / 11
Do

Lead with Neutral Backgrounds

Every LP page background should be one of these four. Never a saturated color.

Pure White #FFFFFF Hero, cards, main content
Warm Off-White #F8F6F2 Section fills, alternating rows
Light Gray #EDEDEC Dividers, input backgrounds
Near-Black #1A1C22 Footer, dark sections, overlays
05 / 11
Do

Reserve Saturation for Impact

High-saturation color earns attention. Spend it only on elements that need to convert.

Primary Buttons
CTAs that trigger the next conversion step
Form Focus States
Active input borders and selection highlights
Prices & Stats
Listing prices, key metrics, social proof numbers
Active Nav Items
Current page indicator in navigation
Alert States
Error messages, availability flags, urgency signals
Key Badges
New listing, featured, sold — high-signal labels only
06 / 11
Do

Maintain WCAG-Grade Contrast

WCAG AA minimum: 4.5:1 for body text · 3:1 for large text and UI components

Dark on Light Property details, descriptions, and all body copy must use near-black on white or off-white backgrounds.
Charcoal on White 12.6:1 ✓
Light on Dark Footer text, dark hero overlays, and modal backgrounds require near-white on near-black.
Cream on Near-Black 9.4:1 ✓
07 / 11
Don't

Avoid Saturated Full-Bleed Sections

Bold colors as section fills overwhelm content and destroy luxury perception.

Section background
✗Electric blue full-bleed — dominates, fights content, kills brand tone
Section background
✗Saturated red fill — jarring, low contrast for text, aggressive
Use instead

Neutral bg (#F8F6F2 or #1A1C22) · accent color only for a single CTA or element within the section

08 / 11
Don't

The 10–15% Accent Rule

Your brand accent should cover no more than 10–15% of the visible page at any scroll position.

55%
Way too much — accent fights neutral for dominance and loses
11%
Just right — accent pops because it's surrounded by negative space
09 / 11
Don't

Max 2 Accent Colors

More than two distinct accent hues fragment visual hierarchy and dilute brand identity.

✗ Don't

5 accents on one page — competing CTAs, no hierarchy, nowhere for the eye to settle

✓ Do

1 accent + neutrals — clear hierarchy, the gold CTA draws the eye every time

Grays, whites, and blacks are structural — they don't count toward the accent limit.

10 / 11
Compliance

Brokerage Color Compliance

Some clients have mandated palettes. Understand the constraint before you design a single pixel.

Compass Strictly monochrome — black, white, and grays only. No brand accent colors permitted anywhere on the site. Strict
Sotheby's Mandated brand palette — deep navy (#002244) as primary, specific warm accents. No substitutions. Strict
Douglas Elliman Navy primary, defined secondary palette. Bold deviations require brand approval — treat as strict. Moderate
Independent Typically flexible — follow the agent's brand kit or LP's default palette recommendations. Flexible

Quick Reference

Color Rules at a Glance

✓ Do
  • ✓Use brand accent for CTAs and active states only
  • ✓Lead with neutral backgrounds — white, off-white, near-black
  • ✓Reserve saturation for high-conversion UI moments
  • ✓Maintain 4.5:1 contrast ratio (WCAG AA minimum)
  • ✓Keep accent coverage under 10–15% of the page
  • ✓Always check brokerage compliance before designing
✗ Don't
  • ✗Use saturated colors as section or hero backgrounds
  • ✗Exceed 15% accent coverage on any viewport
  • ✗Use more than 2 distinct accent hues on one page
  • ✗Place similar tones side by side without contrast
  • ✗Override mandated brokerage color guidelines
  • ✗Use light gray text on white or dark gray on black
