DOHero Section

Lead with a
Strong Hero

The hero section sets the tone for the entire site. It should be high-res, properly cropped, and make an immediate visual impact. This is not the place to save space or cut corners on image quality.

√Ę‚ā¨‚ÄĚ
Full-bleed: edge to edge, no surrounding white space
The hero commands the full viewport. Any containment or visible margin around the hero image signals "template" immediately.
√Ę‚ā¨‚ÄĚ
High-res, intentional crop at every breakpoint
What works on desktop may cut off the subject on mobile. Define a deliberate focal point for each breakpoint √Ę‚ā¨‚ÄĚ never rely on auto-crop.
√Ę‚ā¨‚ÄĚ

The hero is not a content section. It's a first impression. Every word and button that gets added competes with the visual impact you worked to create.

DOContent Architecture

Maintain Clear
Hierarchy on Every Page

Hero √Ę‚Ä†‚Äô Intro/Value Prop √Ę‚Ä†‚Äô Supporting Content √Ę‚Ä†‚Äô Social Proof √Ę‚Ä†‚Äô CTA. Each section should have one clear purpose. Mixing purposes in one section creates confusion.

One purpose per section
A section is either introducing, building trust, showcasing work, gathering leads, or prompting action. Never two at once. When a section tries to do everything, it does nothing effectively.
Sequential logic
Build trust before asking for action. Introduce before showcasing. Social proof before CTA. When sections are out of order, the visitor's confidence never builds to the point of converting.
Label the purpose internally
Before building any section, ask: "What is the one job of this section?" If you can't answer in one sentence, the section needs to be split or cut.
Navigate the page without reading
A well-structured page should be scannable at a glance. If someone skimming at 20% speed can't identify each section's purpose, the hierarchy has failed.
